Profesional 1: Daniel Razmjou

Career Journey

  • Has always been interested in working with technology, configuring systems, analyzing requirements, and understanding the software and hardware of computers.
  • Majored in CIS, but it started in computer science. He enjoyed the utilities and security aspects of CIS and wasn’t too interested in the development side of technology.
  • Where is he at now:
    • Works in IT assurance.
    • Daily work varies, from talking to clients on the phone to ensuring assets follow protocols and are compliant.
    • Workflow: stakeholders present a product/service/task, requirements/issue analysis, provide recommendations, and ensure the problems are solved promptly.

Advice for Students

  • We, as students, have the most time to grow and expand our knowledge and skills.
  • Use available resources to self-learn and perform exercises that will develop your technical skills.
  • A basic understanding of security will benefit other business areas, e.g., accounting, finance, and management.
  • We as students should participate in organizations that grow our expertise in this field, whether in small or professional events.

Memorable Quotes

  • “There is always a need in this field, and not only cybersecurity, but also IT.”
  • “Everything is a project.”
  • “95 to 99 percent of projects aren’t completed on time or don’t even start.”

Profesional 2: Arun Aryal

Career Journey

  • Started as a project manager and wasn’t focusing on cybersecurity.
  • Over time, he was presented with and executed a risk management project and conducted consulting work.
  • Grew his knowledge and expertise in cybersecurity, leading him to teach project management and security courses.
  • Where is he at now:
    • He is mainly a professor and has been for the last 15 years
    • Despite now teaching, his time as a project manager led him to teaching
      • Other responsibilities included understanding the company’s strategies, values, and assets and creating reports and recommendations.

Advice for Students

  • Networking in or outside class (ISACA or professional events) increases opportunities and experiences, whether landing a job from referrals or gaining recognition with new people.
  • Performing hands-on activities allows you to put what you’ve learned into action.
  • Work on taking and obtaining certifications, stating that they boost your knowledge and confidence in your desired field.

Memorable Quotes

  • “Information Systems is IT and management combined.”
  • “Project management bridges the business and technical side of a company.”
