Definition
Cybersecurity professionals plan and implement security measures to protect computer networks and systems from unauthorized access by using various technologies, processes and policies, while also ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
Role
Key roles include:
  • Threat protection & monitoring
  • Identity & access management (IAM)
  • Compliance & risk management
  • Developing ways for businesses to secure IT infrastructure
Responsibilities & Daily Activities
Core responsibilities & daily activities may include:
  • Monitoring network and systems for unusual/suspicious activity
  • Identifying and patching vulnerabilities
  • Conducting regular risk assessments
  • Configuring firewalls, encryption, and other security measures
  • Testing for vulnerabilities through system logs and network traffic
  • Developing incident response plans
  • Training & educating employees on best practices
  • Keeping up to date with new vulnerabilities, zero-day exploits, and malware
Key Technologies & Tools Used
  • Intrusion Detection and Prevention Systems (IDPS)
  • Virtual Private Networks (VPNs)
  • Penetration Testing Tools (Kali Linux, Metasploit, etc.)
  • Password Auditing and Pack Sniffers (Wireshark, Tcpdump, etc.)
  • Network Defense Tools (Netstumbler, etc.)
  • Tools for scanning vulnerabilities, detecting network intrusions, monitoring network security, and encryption (Nmap, Snort, Splunk, Tor, etc.)
